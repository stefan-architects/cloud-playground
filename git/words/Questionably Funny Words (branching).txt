## 📧 Boring Email Phrases People Use Constantly  
These are the phrases that make you sigh before you even open the message.

- **“Per my last email…”** — corporate for “I already told you this.”  
- **“Circling back…”** — the boomerang of passive aggression  
- **“Just checking in…”** — I need something, but I’m pretending I don’t  
- **“As discussed…”** — you forgot, didn’t you  
- **“Hope this finds you well.”** — it never does  
- **“Please advise.”** — I’m done thinking about this  
- **“Not sure if you saw my previous message…”** — you definitely saw it  
- **“Let’s take this offline.”** — we’re about to argue  
- **“Looping in…”** — here comes someone who didn’t ask for this  
- **“Touching base…”** — baseball, but boring  
- **“Moving forward…”** — you messed up, but we’re pretending you didn’t  
- **“In the interest of transparency…”** — something bad happened  
- **“Thanks in advance.”** — you’re doing this whether you want to or not  
- **“Friendly reminder…”** — it’s not friendly  
- **“At your earliest convenience.”** — do it now  
- **“Let me know if you have any questions.”** — please don’t  
- **“I’ll circle back with the team.”** — I’m stalling  
- **“Please see attached.”** — you forgot the attachment  
- **“Following up on this.”** — I refuse to let this die  
- **“Best,”** — the universal sign-off of emotional neutrality
