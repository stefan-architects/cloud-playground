✔️ Milestone 1: Seeing the Shift

(Learning It (again?), Dealing With It (still?), and Accepting That This Is Apparently My Life Now)

Milestone 1 is the moment I realize the cloud isn’t just something I built — it’s something I now have to live with.  
Everything was peaceful when it was just provisioning: templates, stacks, IAM roles, VPCs — all tidy, all theoretical.

But now things are live, and suddenly I’m in a relationship with my infrastructure.

The shift looks like this:

- “I built the thing.”  
turns into  
- “Why is the thing doing that?”

✔️ The Beginner Cloud‑Practitioner Checklist

(Learning It (again?), Dealing With It (still?), and Hoping Today Isn’t “One of Those Days”)

---

🌱 Mindset Shift
-  ] [Admit this is a checklist — I’m new, I need structure, and checking boxes helps me pretend I understand what’s happening.  
-  ] [Shift from provisioning to operations — Cloud work isn’t “deploy and walk away.” It’s “deploy, observe, troubleshoot, and occasionally whisper ‘please behave’ at CloudWatch.”

---

🔍 Operational Awareness
-  ] [Keep an eye on what’s running — Logs, metrics, dashboards, alerts — I’m watching them all like a raccoon watching a campsite. Curious. Concerned. Slightly chaotic.  
-  ] [Respond when things get weird — I’m learning to investigate issues, triage problems, and escalate when needed. I don’t have to fix everything — just understand why the cloud is being dramatic.

---

🛠️ Tools & Daily Workflow
-  ] [Get comfortable with the CLI and CloudFormation — The AWS CLI still looks like a spellbook. CloudFormation updates still feel like defusing a bomb. But I’m learning. Slowly. Loudly.  
-  ] [Use the tools that show you reality — CloudWatch, Config, Health Dashboard — my new trio of “what fresh chaos is this?”

---

📁 Repo & Code Reality
-  ] [Keep the repo organized (or at least try) — Naming things is hard. Organizing things is harder. But I’m doing my best to keep the repo from becoming a cryptid.  
-  ] [Maintain infrastructure code — Updating templates, cleaning up unused resources, and pretending I totally meant to deploy that change.

---

🧠 Future‑You Maintenance
-  ] [Document and automate — I’m writing things down because future‑me deserves mercy. I’m automating tasks because clicking the same button 12 times is a crime against humanity.  
-  ] [Capture what you learn — Every weird issue teaches me something. I’m saving those lessons so I don’t have to relearn them at 3 AM.

---

⏳ Expectations & Progress
-  ] [Be patient with progress — Some days I feel like a cloud wizard. Other days I stare at a metric spike like it personally betrayed me.  
-  ] [Understand that operations is ongoing — There is no “done.” There is only “less confused than yesterday.”

---

🌩️ Bonus: Accept the Chaos (Gently)
-  ] [Roll with the weird moments — Sometimes the cloud does something unexpected. Sometimes I do something unexpected. We’re both learning.  
-  ] [Expect surprises — The cloud is basically a polite chaos engine. I’m learning to navigate it with curiosity instead of panic.
