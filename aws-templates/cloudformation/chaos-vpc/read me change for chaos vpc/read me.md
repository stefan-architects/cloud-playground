✔️ Milestone 1: Seeing the Shift

Milestone 1 is the moment you realize cloud work isn’t just provisioning anymore.  
Up to now, everything I built was theoretical — templates, stacks, IAM roles, VPCs, all neatly deployed.

But now things are live.

And when things go live, the job shifts from:

- “I built the thing.”  
to  
- “Now I have to understand the thing, watch the thing, and keep the thing alive!!!”

✔️ The Beginner Cloud‑Practitioner Checklist

(Learning It, Dealing With It, and Pretending Everything Is Fine)

---

🌱 Mindset Shift
-  ] [Admit this is a checklist — I need structure, and checking boxes makes me feel like I’m keeping the cloud (and my sanity) together.  
-  ] [Shift from provisioning to operations — Cloud work isn’t “deploy and disappear.” It’s “deploy, observe, maintain, troubleshoot, and occasionally whisper ‘please behave’ at CloudWatch.”

🔍 Operational Awareness
-  ] [Keep an eye on what’s running — I’m watching logs, metrics, dashboards, and alerts. Sometimes I understand them. Sometimes they look like the cloud is sending me riddles. Is this normal?  
-  ] [Respond when things get weird — I’m learning to investigate issues, triage problems, and escalate when needed. I don’t have to fix everything — just understand what’s happening.

🛠️ Tools & Daily Workflow
-  ] [Get comfortable with the CLI and CloudFormation — The AWS CLI and CloudShell still look like spellbooks, but I’m learning the incantations. CloudFormation updates feel scary, but I’m learning to trust the process (and read the change sets).  
-  ] [Use the tools that show you reality — CloudWatch, Config, Health Dashboard — these are becoming my new weather apps.

📁 Repo & Code Reality
-  ] [Keep the repo organized (or at least try) — I’m naming things, structuring folders, and trying not to create chaos. The repo is slowly becoming less “mystery box” and more “okay, I see what’s happening here.”  
-  ] [Maintain infrastructure code — Updating templates, cleaning up unused resources, and keeping things consistent is part of the job now.

🧠 Future‑You Maintenance
-  ] [Document and automate — I’m writing things down because future‑me deserves kindness. I’m automating repetitive tasks because clicking the same button 12 times feels like a personal attack.  
-  ] [Capture what you learn — Every weird issue teaches me something. I’m saving those lessons so I don’t relearn them the hard way.

⏳ Expectations & Progress
-  ] [Be patient with progress — Some days everything makes sense. Other days I stare at CloudWatch like it personally offended me. Learning cloud is a journey, not a sprint.  
-  ] [Understand that operations is ongoing — There’s no “done.” There’s only “better than yesterday.”

🌩️ Bonus: Accept the Chaos (Gently)
-  ] [Roll with the weird moments — Sometimes the cloud does something unexpected. Sometimes I do something unexpected. Either way, we learn, adjust, and keep going.  
-  ] [Expect surprises — The cloud is basically a polite chaos engine. I’m learning to navigate it with curiosity instead of panic.
